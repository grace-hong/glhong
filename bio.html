About

Originally from the Midwest, Grace cares deeply about democratizing entrepreneurship and catalyzing innovation.
Prior to her current role, Grace invested at BBG Ventures (early-stage) and Summit Partners (growth equity), traded at J.P. Morgan, and programmed at Microsoft.
Grace graduated from Princeton University with a degree in Economics and certificates in Computer Science and Tech & Society. At Princeton, she was involved with the Center for Information Technology Policy and the Julis-Rabinowitz Center for Public Policy and Finance. She also was a research assistant in the School of Public and International Affairs.
She served as the President of Business Today, a 501(c)3 non-profit that seeks to bridge the gap between students and executives. During her term, she oversaw a million-dollar budget, raised over a half a million to support operations, and recruited the participation of executives including the CEO of Citigroup (Jane Fraser), CEO of Y Combinator (Michael Seibel), and CEO of AstraZeneca (Pascal Soriot). She now advises undergraduates as a member of Business Today's trustee board.
Grace currently lives and works in New York. In her free time, she mentors for Big Sibs and reviews films for Rooftop Films annual festival.
Otherwise, she can be found biking to a new coffee shop, music-making with friends, or reading in the park.
